Lily Tomlin Biography

Lily Tomlin is a famous American actress, comedian, writer, singer, and producer. She started her career as a stand-up comedian and as well performing off-Broadway during the 1960s.

Her getaway role was on the variety show Rowan & Martin’s Laugh-In from 1969 until 1973. She starred as Frankie Bergstein on the Netflix series Grace and Frankie, in which she made her first performance in 2015 and gained nominations for four Primetime Emmy Awards, three Screen Actors Guild Awards, and a Golden Globe Award.

    Lily Tomlin Education

    Tomlin studied and graduated from Cass Technical High School in 1957. She enrolled at Wayne State University and initially studied biology. She auditioned for a play, and it flashed her interest in a career in the theatre and she switched her major. After college, Tomlin started doing stand-up comedy in nightclubs in Detroit and later in New York City.

    She resumed studying acting at the HB Studio. In 1965, Lily made her first television appearance on The Merv Griffin Show. One year later, she became a cast member on the short-lived third and final incarnation of The Garry Moore Show.

    Lily Tomlin Family

    Tomlin was born in Detroit, Michigan to her caring mother Lillie Mae (née Ford; January 14, 1914 – July 12, 2005), and her father, Guy Tomlin (March 3, 1913 – October 24, 1970). Her mother was a housewife and nurse’s aide while her father was a factory worker. She grew up together with her younger brother, Richard Tomlin. Her parents were Southern Baptists who transferred to Detroit from Paducah, Kentucky, during the Great Depression.
