Introduction 

As of January 2024, Scottie Pippen’s net worth is roughly $20 Million.

Scottie Pippen is an American former NBA basketball player who played a total of 17 seasons in the NBA.

He has won six NBA championships with the Chicago Bulls.

Along with legend Michael Jordan, he is known for being one of the best NBA players in history.

He was named the All-NBA First Team three times. 

Early Life

Scotty Maurice Pippen was born on the 25th of September, 1965, in Arkansas.

Pippen is the youngest child of the 12 children in the family.

His passion for basketball developed at an early age.

When he was younger he aspired to make the winning shot in the 7th game of an NBA championship on the same day.

He attended Hamburg High School where he played for the school basketball team. 

Career 

Before becoming one of the richest athletes in the world, Scottie Pippen played for the University of Central Arkansas after being discovered as a walk-on. 

While he didn’t receive much media coverage, as a senior he grew to stand 6ft 8 in and averaged 23.6 points per game. 

Pippen’s skills earned him NAIA All-American honors in ‘86 and ‘87, making him the dominant player in the Arkansas Intercollegiate Conference. 

His performance in the Conference earned him the attention of NBA Scouts, which prompted him to join the 1987 Draft. 

A Professional Rookie

During the 1987 NBA Draft, Scottie Pippen was grabbed by the Chicago Bulls, who had been eyeing him since his college days. 

Pippen became part of the Bulls’ young forward duo alongside Horace Grant, and he made his rookie debut in November 1987. 

In his first season, legend Michael Jordan served as Pippen’s mentor, and his skills improved dramatically for it, as they played one-on-one outside team practices. 

During the 1988 NBA Playoffs, Pippen took the starting small forward position, helping the Bulls reach the semifinals for the first time in over a decade. 

He continued to improve as the Bulls reached the Eastern Conference finals in ‘89 and ‘90, though they were beaten by the Detroit Pistons. 

A Rising Star

In the 1990-91 NBA season, Pippen became the Bulls’ primary defensive stopper, and he had his first triple-double in November. 

Pippen led the team in blocks per game and was ranked fifth overall in the NBA for steals, earning him NBA All-Defensive Second Team honors. 

With his help, the Chicago Bulls went on to defeat Magic Johnson and the Los Angeles Lakers in the 1991 NBA Finals. 

Following this, he went on to help the Bulls achieve their first three-peat, winning the Finals two more times consecutively in ‘92 and ‘93. 

Moving On Without Jordan 

Before the 1993-94 season, Michael Jordan retired, and thanks to his absence, Pippen was able to truly shine on his own for the first time. 

During that season, he earned All-Star Game MVP honors and led the Bulls in scoring, assists, and blocks, while being second in the NBA for steals. 

In Game 6, when Patrick Ewing jumped to block his shot, Pippen dunked the ball while taking Ewing’s hand in his face. 

This soon became one of Pippen’s signature career plays and one of the most iconic moments in NBA history. 

Pippen finished the 1994 season leading the Bulls in every category, joining Dave Cowens as one of the few players in history to achieve the feat. 

A Second Three-Peat

With the return of Michael Jordan and the addition of Dennis Rodman, the Bulls were able to post their best regular season record. 

During the 1996-97 season, both Jordan and Pippen were selected to be among the 50 Greatest Players in NBA History. 

The following season ended up becoming the Chicago Bulls’ second three-peat, when they ran the 1998 NBA Finals, besting the Utah Jazz. 

However, that season also proved to be Pippen’s last with the team, as general manager Jerry Krause sought to trade him. 

Rockets And Trail Blazers

After eleven years with the Bulls, Scottie Pippen was traded to the Houston Rockets in January 1999, in exchange for Roy Rogers in a sign-and-trade deal. 

To pay for Pippen, Charles Barkey made a lot of sacrifices to grant him an $11 million salary, four times more than his previous salary. 

On the court, Pippen was named to the NBA All-Defensive First-Team for the eighth time and helped the Rockets finish fifth in the Western Conference. 

However, after the season, Pippen requested to be traded, a move Barkley criticized him for, stating he was “disappointed” in the all-star. 

Pippen joined the Portland Trailer Blazers in October 1999 and played for them until 2003, during which time he continued to stand out. 

Playing With Legends 

As a member of the Trail Blazers, Scottie Pippen faced off against the All-Star Duo of Kobe Bryant and Shaquille O’Neal of the Lakers. 

In his first season, the pair overtook Pippen’s lead to knock the Trail Blazers out of the Finals to become the 2000 NBA Champions. 

The following season, Pippen suffered from tendonitis in his elbow, which required surgery and caused him to struggle on the court. 

When the season was over, he re-signed with the Chicago Bulls for the 2003-04 season, which would be his last. 

Pippen played his final NBA career game against the Seattle SuperSonics in February 2004, and soon after, he announced his retirement. 

An Executive Comeback

While he made a brief comeback with the Finnish league team Torpan Pojat in 2008, in 2010 he returned to the Bulls as a team advisor. 

In 2012, he was named the Bulls’ senior advisor by the team’s president, Michael Reinsdorf, a role that he kept until 2020. 

Now one of the richest NBA players in the world, Pippen is set to appear at the Bulls Ring of Honor event in 2024 alongside Jordan and Rodman.
