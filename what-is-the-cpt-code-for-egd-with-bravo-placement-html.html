What exactly is an EDG?

An esophagogastroduodenoscopy (EGD) is a procedure in which your doctor examines the lining of your oesophagus, stomach, and duodenum via a scope. The oesophagus is a muscular tube that links your neck to your stomach and the duodenum, which is the top section of your small intestine. It is responsible for digestion. An endoscope is a tiny camera that is attached to a tube.

Is an EGD regarded to be surgical procedure?

In an endoscopic procedure, your doctor may check the oesophagus, stomach, and duodenum, among other organs in your body (part of your small intestine). EGD is an outpatient operation, which means you will be able to return home the same day.
