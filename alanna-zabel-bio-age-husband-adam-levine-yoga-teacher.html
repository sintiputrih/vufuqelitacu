Alanna Zabel Career

Alanna Zabel started her career as an actress. Then she shifted towards yoga leaving behind her dreams to become a movie star. She told The LA Girl, “I decided to get out of the acting industry because I started seeing parallels with the “characters” I played and my real life. I decided to only play “me”: Alanna Zabel – I AM became AZIAM.”

Alanna founded AZ I AM, an ERYT-500 certified yoga, pilates, and fitness instructor with 25 years of experience. She is also the creator of Yoga Barre®, a registered ERYT-500 yoga instructor, Pilates, and fitness instructor.

In 2000, she established her own business, YogaDance, which fuses essential movements of yoga with contemporary dance. She has also performed on stage at numerous fashion exhibitions and conferences. She also performed at a private party held at Michael Jackson’s house and performed at Neverland.

Furthermore, Alanna’s performances have been requested at fashion shows, conferences, and private parties.

The yogi also delved into creating a clothing line which she launched as the premium AZ I AM® Active Wear collections. Beyond her accomplishments as an instructor, performer, program director, writer, and designer, Alanna is a creative force and a teacher with a passion for kids. In addition to fourteen children’s books with yoga and wellness themes, she single-handedly created The World’s First Yoga Doll® and Soul Model collections.

In 2011, Alanna was voted the “Best Yoga Instructor in Los Angeles” by LA Family Magazine. Shape Magazine also voted “Hollywood’s Hottest Trainers” in 2014. She is passionate about teaching yoga through her classes, life, and writing. She has authored books titled  As I Am: Where Spirituality Meets Reality and Meaningful Coincidence: Synchronistic Stories of the Soul.

As one of the most sought-after instructors in the country, Alanna’s clients have included everyone from Hollywood executives and busy working moms, to 2005’s NBA Rookie of the Year Emeka Okafor.

Alanna is a graduate of Hamburg High School and Studied Theatre & Dance at the University at Buffalo and graduated in 1994.
