Biography of Dillon Brooks

Canadian professional basketball player Dillon Brooks plays for the Memphis Grizzlies of the National Basketball Association (NBA).

He was the Pac-12 Player of the Year and a unanimous second-team All-American while playing NCAA basketball for the Oregon Ducks in 2017.

He competes for Canada’s national team. Findlay Preparatory School provided Brooks, who was born on January 22, 1996, with his foundational education. His next trip was Father Henry Carr Catholic Secondary School as he started to fall in love with court sports. 2014 saw his enrollment in the University of Oregon, and 2017 saw his graduation.

The Houston Rockets selected Brooks with the 45th overall choice in the 2017 NBA Draft. After being selected, Brooks was traded to the Memphis Grizzlies. In the Grizzlies’ 2017 season opener game, Brooks made his NBA debut. In his first game, he scored 19, which is thought to be the most points ever scored by a Canadian player in their NBA debut. During his 2019 season, he experienced a ruptured ligament problem, which prevented him from playing in the remaining games of the season.

Brooks averaged 16.1 points per game in the 2019–20 season, which was his prime. The Grizzlies extended his deal by 3 years/$35 million after his outstanding season. On May 23, when Brooks entered the NBA playoffs, he scored a career-high 31 points. He struggled with injuries throughout the subsequent seasons, missing a lot of games.
