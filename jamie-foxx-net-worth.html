Jamie Foxx Net Worth : Career &amp; Lifestyle

The American actor, comedian, songwriter, singer, and record producer Jamie Foxx’s net worth is $150 million as of December, 2023.

Foxx became widely known for his role in the 2004 biographical film Ray as Ray Charles.

He won several awards for his portrayal, including the Academy Award, Screen Actors Guild Award, Golden Globe Award, BAFTA, and Critics Choice Movie Award for best actor in the leading role.

With that many award-winning from a single movie, he became the second actor to win all five major lead actor awards for the same performance and only one to win a Golden Globe for the non-drama category.

Apart from Academy Award-winning biopic, Foxx has appeared in some critically acclaimed movies in his career, including Django Unchained and Just Mercy.

Apart from movies, Foxx is also known for his music career. He has produced four studio albums which have charted in the top ten of U.S billboard 200.

Four albums of Grammy-winning musicians are “Unpredictable,” which topped the chart, “Intuition,”  “Best Night of My Life” and, 2015 Hollywood: “A Story of a Dozen Roses.”

Similarly, Fox has served as the host, and executive producer of the Foxx game show Beat Shazam since spring 2017.

Jamie Foxx: Early Life

Originally, Eric Marlon Bishop, Jamie Foxx was born on December the 13th, 1967, in Terrell, Texas. His father, Darrell Bishop, was a stockbroker, and his mother was Louise Annette Talley Dixon, a housewife.

Jamie Foxx was raised by his mother’s adoptive parents shortly after he was born. His biological parents were not part of his upbringing, but he has had little contact with them.

Jamie was raised in the black quarter of Terrell, which at the time was a racially separated community. Foxx admits that his grandmother’s influence in his life was one of the main reasons for his success.

Foxx started playing piano when he was just five years of age. He had a severe Baptist upbringing, and as a teenager, he played piano part-time in a choir in Terrell’s New Hope Baptist Church.

His natural gift for telling jokes was already in indication as a third-grader when his teacher would use him as a prize: if the class acted well, Foxx would tell them jokes.

Jamie went to Terrell High School, where he played basketball and football as a quarterback. He was not just elite in sports but also received top grades.

Foxx always wanted to play for Dallas Cowboys, and interestingly he was the first player in the school’s history to pass for more than 1,000 yards.

He also had a band named Leather and Lace, in which he was a frontman. After he was done with high school, Jamie received a scholarship to United States International University. He studied musical and performing arts composition.
