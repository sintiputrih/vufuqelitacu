Early Life and Career

Jaron Lanier was born in New York City and raised in Mesilla, New Mexico. He attended New Mexico State University before dropping out to pursue his interests in the field of technology. Lanier’s career took off in the 1980s when he co-founded VPL Research, a company that developed and sold VR goggles and gloves.

Lanier’s contributions to the field of virtual reality are significant. He is credited with coining the term ‘virtual reality’ and has developed various VR technologies. His work in this field has significantly contributed to his net worth.

Music and Writing Career

His books, including “You Are Not a Gadget” and “Who Owns the Future?”, have been translated into many languages and have sold millions of copies worldwide.
