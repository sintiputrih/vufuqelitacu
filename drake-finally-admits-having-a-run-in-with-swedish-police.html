Drakes Admission About His Encounter with Swedish Police


After initially denying the incident, Drake has now confirmed that he had a run-in with Swedish police.

The Canadian rapper shared a series of photos on Sunday, July 17, chronicling his recent experiences.

Among the images he posted was a picture of a document given to him by Swedish authorities, also known as Polisen.

The document is titled “Information for Those Suspected of a Crime and Subsequently Detained.”

It outlines Drake’s rights under Swedish law, such as the right to be informed of the reasons for his detention and the right to legal representation.

Drake’s response follows speculation that he was arrested on marijuana-related charges while attending a party at a Swedish club this week.

This led to “Free Drake” trending on social media.

Although his team initially denied the incident, the rapper has now acknowledged the truth of the matter.
