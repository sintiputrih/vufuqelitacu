What is Ali Larter’s Net Worth?

Ali Larter, a renowned American model and actress, has a net worth of $12 million. She is best known for her roles in popular film franchises such as “Final Destination” and “Resident Evil” and her appearance in the NBC television series “Heroes”.

She started her career as a model, being discovered by a scout on the streets of New Jersey at the age of 14. After signing with Ford Modeling Agency, she worked in the US, Australia, Italy, and Japan. However, after moving to Los Angeles, she started taking acting classes and started booking guest roles on TV shows like “Suddenly Susan”, “Dawson’s Creek”, and “Just Shoot Me!”.

She then transitioned to the big screen, appearing in movies such as “Varsity Blues”, “Drive Me Crazy”, “Final Destination”, and “Legally Blonde”. It wasn’t until 2006 when she became a household name, appearing in the hit series “Heroes” as Niki Sanders and later as Tracy Strauss.

As a model, Larter gained recognition for her portrayal of fictional celebrity Allegra Coleman in a 1996 hoax in Esquire magazine.

Early Life and Education

Ali Larter was born in Cherry Hill, New Jersey on February 28, 1976. Her parents were Margaret, a realtor, and Danforth, a trucking executive. She attended Carusi Middle School and Cherry Hill High School West, but did not complete her senior year due to her modeling career.

Early Career and Modeling

At the age of 14, Ali Larter was discovered by a modeling scout and starred in a commercial for the Philadelphia Phillies. This led to a contract with the Ford Modeling Agency in New York. She modeled in various countries including Australia, Japan, and Italy.

In 1996, Larter gained fame for her portrayal of fictional celebrity Allegra Coleman for a hoax feature in Esquire magazine. Larter appeared on the cover of the magazine as this imaginary woman, created by writer Martha Sherrill to satirize celebrity profiles.

Ali Larter’s Film Career

Ali Larter made her feature film debut in 1999 in “Varsity Blues” and later that year had supporting roles in “Giving it Up,” “Drive Me Crazy,” and “House on Haunted Hill.” In 2000, she starred in “Final Destination” and its sequel. Larter also appeared in “Legally Blonde,” “Jay and Silent Bob Strike Back,” and “American Outlaws.” In 2004, she starred in “Three Way” and in 2005, she appeared in “Confess” and “A Lot Like Love.” In 2007, Larter had four film credits, including “Homo Erectus,” “Crazy,” “Marigold,” and “Resident Evil: Extinction.” She reprised her role in the “Resident Evil” franchise in two later installments. Other notable film credits include “Obsessed,” “Lovesick,” “The Diabolical,” and “The Last Victim.”

Television Career

Ali Larter made her television debut in 1997 with appearances in episodes of “Suddenly Susan” and “Chicago Sons.” She went on to appear in episodes of “Chicago Hope,” “Just Shoot Me!,” and “Dawson’s Creek” in 1998. Larter’s biggest television role came in 2006, when she played Niki Sanders on the NBC science-fiction series “Heroes.” She played another character named Tracy Strauss in the third season. After “Heroes,” Larter had main roles on the TNT crime drama “Legends” and the Fox baseball drama “Pitch.” She also appeared in episodes of “Curb Your Enthusiasm” and “Splitting Up Together.” Larter played the recurring part of Dr. Grace Sawyer in the second season of the police procedural series “The Rookie” from 2019 to 2020.

Modeling and Other Appearances

Ali Larter has continued to model for various magazines such as Glamour, Seventeen, Maxim, Allure, and Cosmopolitan. She has also appeared in ads for brands like Stolichnaya, Parfums de Coeur, and Estée Lauder.

In 2010, Larter participated in the “Women Deliver” United Nations conference in Washington, DC. In 2013, she and her husband hosted the Art of Elysium Gala in Los Angeles.

Marriage and Family

In 2007, Larter became engaged to Hayes MacArthur, an actor and comedian with whom she had been in a long-term relationship. The couple tied the knot in 2009, and they have since welcomed two children into their family.

Real Estate Transactions

Ali Larter and her husband Hayes MacArthur purchased a home in Santa Monica, California for just under $4 million in 2017. Prior to this, the couple owned a home in the Hollywood Hills which they bought in 2009 for $3 million and sold in 2016 for $4.15 million.
