Nicole Ari Parker Net Worth

What Is Nicole Ari Parker’s Net Worth and Salary?

Nicole Ari Parker is a successful American actress, producer, and model with a combined net worth of $5 million with her husband, fellow actor Boris Kodjoe.

Parker is best known for her role as Teri Joseph on the Showtime series “Soul Food” and has acted in over 60 films and television shows throughout her career. Some of her notable works include “Boogie Nights,” “Remember the Titans,” and “Brown Sugar.”

In addition to acting, Parker has also executive produced “The Boris & Nicole Show” and the TV movie “Downsized.”

Overall, Nicole Ari Parker is a talented and accomplished figure in the entertainment industry, with a wide range of experience and success in her career.

Nicole Ari Parker Early Life

Nicole Ari Parker was born on October 7, 1970, in Baltimore, Maryland. She attended a Montessori school before transferring to Roland Park Country School. At 17, Parker was named Best Actress in Maryland’s high school theater competition. She went on to join The Washington Ballet Company and earn an acting degree from NYU’s Tisch School of the Arts in 1993.

Her parents, Donald (a dentist) and Susan (a health care professional), are divorced.

Nicole Ari Parker’s Acting Career

Nicole Ari Parker made her television debut in “Other Women’s Children” and went on to appear in films such as “Boogie Nights,” “Blue Streak,” and “Remember the Titans.” She starred in the TV series “Soul Food” and had recurring roles on shows like “Murder in the First” and “Empire.” Parker also appeared in the films “Almost Christmas” and “How It Ends.” In 2021, she played Lisa Todd Wexley on the “Sex and the City” revival “And Just Like That….”

Marriages and Family

Nicole Ari Parker was previously married to actor Joseph Falasca in 2001, but they divorced later that year. She then married Boris Kodjoe, her co-star from “Soul Food,” in Germany on May 21, 2005. The couple has two children together: daughter Sophie, who was born on March 5, 2005, and son Nicolas, who was born on October 31, 2006. Sophie was diagnosed with spina bifida at birth.

Boris Kodjoe was a series regular on “Real Husbands of Hollywood” and co-hosted “The Boris & Nicole Show” with Parker in 2015.

Awards and Nominations

Nicole Ari Parker won a Special Award at the Urbanworld Film Festival in 1999 for her outstanding body of work as an actress. She also shared a Florida Film Critics Circle Award for Best Ensemble Cast and a Screen Actors Guild Award nomination for Outstanding Performance by a Cast with her “Boogie Nights” co-stars in 1998.

Parker earned seven NAACP Image Award nominations: Outstanding Actress in a Drama Series for “Soul Food” (2001, 2002, 2003, 2004, and 2005) and Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Motion Picture for “Remember the Titans” (2001) and “Brown Sugar” (2003). She also received a BET Award nomination for Best Actress for “Soul Food” and “Brown Sugar” in 2003, and Black Reel Award nominations for Network/Cable – Best Actress for “Dancing in September” (2002) and Outstanding Supporting Actress, TV Movie or Mini-Series for “Pastor Brown” (2014).
