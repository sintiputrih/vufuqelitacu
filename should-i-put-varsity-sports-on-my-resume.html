Should I put varsity sports on my resume?

Creating an impressive resume is essential in today's competitive job market. It's not only about showcasing your academic achievements and professional experience but also highlighting your unique qualities and skills. For many individuals, being a college athlete is a significant part of their lives. The question arises: Should varsity sports be included on your resume? In this article, we will explore the benefits of including your athletic experience on your resume and how it can enhance your employability.

  • Transferable Skills

Being a college athlete requires dedication, discipline, and hard work. These qualities translate into valuable transferable skills that are highly sought after by employers. Collaboration, cooperation, and effective communication are vital in team sports, and these skills can be applied to the workplace. By including your athletic experience on your resume, you demonstrate your ability to work well with others and contribute to a team's success.

  • Leadership and Decision-Making Abilities

Varsity sports provide numerous opportunities to develop leadership and decision-making abilities. As a captain or team leader, you are responsible for guiding and motivating your teammates, making important decisions under pressure, and setting a positive example. These experiences showcase your ability to take charge, think critically, and make sound judgments, all of which are valuable skills in the professional world.

  • Goal-Oriented Mindset

Athletes are familiar with setting goals and working relentlessly towards achieving them. This goal-oriented mindset is highly valuable in the workplace. Including your varsity sports experience on your resume demonstrates your determination, drive, and ability to stay focused on achieving objectives. Employers appreciate candidates who can demonstrate a track record of setting and accomplishing goals, making you a more attractive candidate.

  • Adaptability

Varsity athletes often face unforeseen challenges, such as injuries, schedule changes, or unexpected setbacks. These experiences cultivate adaptability and resilience, which are crucial attributes in today's fast-paced and ever-changing work environment. By including your athletic experience on your resume, you can showcase your ability to thrive under pressure, adapt to new situations, and overcome obstacles.

  • Stress Management Skills

Balancing academics and athletics can be demanding, requiring effective time management and stress management skills. Including your varsity sports experience on your resume demonstrates your ability to handle pressure, manage your time effectively, and perform well in high-stress situations. These skills are highly valued by employers, as they indicate your ability to handle challenging tasks and meet deadlines.

  • Treating Athletic Experience as Work Experience

To maximize the impact of your athletic experience on your resume, it is crucial to treat it as valuable work experience. Provide specific details about your role, responsibilities, and accomplishments. Highlight any leadership positions, awards, or notable achievements. Additionally, emphasize the transferable skills you gained through your athletic endeavors. By presenting your athletic experience in a professional manner, you can effectively communicate the value it brings to potential employers.

  • Seeking Career Development Support

While being a college athlete can undoubtedly enhance your resume, it is essential to seek career development support to maximize your employability. Many universities offer career services tailored specifically to student-athletes. These services can help you explore career options, develop your resume and cover letter, and prepare for interviews. Taking advantage of these resources can help you effectively leverage your athletic experience and bridge the gap between sports and your desired profession.

In conclusion, including your varsity sports experience on your resume can be highly beneficial. It showcases your transferable skills, leadership abilities, goal-oriented mindset, adaptability, and stress management skills. However, it is crucial to treat your athletic experience as valuable work experience, providing specific details and emphasizing the skills gained. By seeking career development support and effectively communicating the value of your athletic experience, you can enhance your employability and stand out to potential employers. So, if you're wondering whether to put varsity sports on your resume, the answer is, "Yes, absolutely!"
