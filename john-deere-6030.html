John Deere 6030 Price, Reviews, Specs, Engine Oil Capacity, Weight, Serial Numbers, Attachments, History, Features & Pictures

Introduced in late 1971, the John Deere 6030 was essentially the 5010/5020 series tractors’ final evolution.

They were both excellent enough tractors, but they looked clumsy and heavy like they couldn’t really get out of their way.

When John Deere 6030 came out, that clumsy image of John Deere’s “big” tractors went away pretty quickly.

John Deere 6030 Row-Crop Tractor is fitted with a John Deere turbocharged inter-cooled diesel 6-cylinder liquid-cooled inline 531 ci [8.7 L] engine, which delivers 148.70 hp [110.9 kW] Drawbar (tested) power at rated rpm 2100.

John Deere 6030 Row-Crop Tractor featured a John Deere 8.7L 6-cyl diesel engine, 4×2 2WD, 5910 lbs [2680 kg] Rear lift, power steering, 8 forward and 2 reverse gears and 18,180 lbs [8246 kg] weight.
