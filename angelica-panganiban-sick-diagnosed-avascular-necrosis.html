Is Angelica Panganiban Sick? Avascular Necrosis Diagnosis

Is Angelica Panganiban sick? Yes, the renowned Filipino-American actress recently disclosed that she has been diagnosed with a bone disease.

Angelica Panganiban is revered for her diverse talent as a Filipino-American actress. In addition to being a skilled performer, the Payatas-born star is also a TV host and comedian.

Renowned as the “Queen of Drama” in the Philippines, her revelation about her health has prompted concern and support from her widespread fanbase.

Today’s article provides more detailed insights into Angelica Panganiban’s journey with avascular necrosis, emphasizing her challenges, decisions, and resilient outlook.

Is Angelica Panganiban Sick? Diagnosed With Avascular Necrosis

In a heartfelt video blog posted on 18 November, Panganiban bravely revealed that she is suffering from avascular necrosis.

It is a serious bone disease that caused her intense pain in her hip area.

According to the Mayo Clinic, the condition, also known as “bone death,” occurs when the blood supply to a bone is cut off, leading to the death of bone tissue and the collapse of the bone structure.

In the video, the actress talked about her diagnosis while lying on a bed, but it was not clear if she was in a hospital or at home.

She said that she first felt the pain, but she thought it was nothing serious. The TV host later experienced more pain while doing physical exercises and yoga.

She then consulted a bone specialist at Asian Hospital, who confirmed that she had avascular necrosis.

Her fans and followers were shocked and saddened by her revelation. They sent her support and prayers, hoping she would recover soon and resume her life and career.

It Started When Angelica Panganiban Was Pregnant

Angelica Panganiban’s journey with this condition began subtly during her pregnancy, manifesting as discomfort around her hips.

Dismissing it initially as typical pregnancy-related issues, the actress continued with her routine. Post-delivery, engaging in physical exercises exacerbated the discomfort, compelling her to attribute it to routine physical strain.

However, during a trip to Palawan, excruciating hip pain immobilized her, prompting immediate medical attention upon her return home.

Her subsequent hospital visit and subsequent therapy revealed the gravity of her ailment, requiring a specialized approach due to her breastfeeding limitations.

Panganiban’s fiancé, Gregg Homan, played a pivotal role, accompanying her to a bone specialist who recommended platelet-rich plasma (PRP) injections.

Diagnostic MRI scans confirmed the diagnosis of avascular necrosis, revealing significant damage to her hip bones, severely impairing her mobility.

Doctor Suggested Joint Replacement

In the same vlog, the prominent TV host revealed that doctors initially suggested her joint replacement surgery. Panganiban, opting for a conservative route, decided on the PRP injections.

The actress, deeply affected by the physical toll and emotional distress, questioned the situation and her condition.

“Why me? What caused this? Nothing,” she lamented, expressing her bewilderment at the unforeseen health challenge.

Despite these uncertainties, Panganiban remains resilient, choosing a positive outlook as she embarks on her treatment journey.

Eager to recover quickly, she aims to resume work by 2024, embracing hope and determination amid this unexpected health setback.

Angelica Panganiban’s transparency about her battle with avascular necrosis exemplifies strength and fortitude.

Her decision for a non-invasive treatment approach and her resolve to confront adversity with positivity inspire many.
