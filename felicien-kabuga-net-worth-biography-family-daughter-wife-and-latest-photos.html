Felicien Kabuga Genocide

Kabuga accumulated his wealth by controlling tea farms in northern Rwanda, among other business ventures. A multimillionaire, he was closely attached to Juvénal Habyarimana’s MRND party and the Akazu, an informal group of Hutu radicals from northern Rwanda that actively participated in the Rwandan genocide.

Kabuga was also purportedly heavily linked in the establishment and funding of RTLM, as well as THE Kangura magazine. In 1993, at an RTLM fundraising gathering planned by the MRND Party, Félicien allegedly openly clarified the goal of RTLM station as the defence of Hutu Power.

Throughout the ICTR’s so-called “media trial”, then RTLM host Georges Ruggiu specified Kabuga as the “Chairman Director-general” of the RTLM station, with obligations such as “presiding over RTLM” and “representing RTLM.”

A total of 500,000 machetes were shipped into Rwanda between January 1993 to March 1994, statistically one for every three adult Hutus in the country at that time. Kabuga has been listed as one of the main importers of those machetes.

Before the genocide happened in 1994, Mr. Kabuga was the President of the National Defence Fund (NDF). Under President Juvenal Habyarimana’s government, Mr. Kabuga had tremendous political publicity and was a powerful financier.
