A Comprehensive Analysis: Top 10 Pokmon Cards by Rarity of All Time and Value

For years we have been trading Pokémon cards – the franchise instilled such a sense of collectability in us all from an early age. With that being said, we will be digging into the thrilling life of Pokémon card collecting in order to provide you with what many would consider the top 10 rarest and most valuable cards ever made.

 

1. Pikachu Illustrator

The Pikachu Illustrator card is one of the most sought-after Pokémon cards due to its rarity and historical significance. Only 39 copies of this card were ever made, and it was awarded to winners of the CoroCoro Comic Illustration Contest in 1997. Today, this card holds an extraordinary value, making it a prized possession for collectors worldwide.

 

2. Charizard First Edition

The first edition Charizard card from the Base Set is an iconic and highly coveted card among Pokémon enthusiasts. Its powerful imagery and scarcity have propelled its value to astonishing heights, making it a must-have for serious collectors.

 

3. Snap Cards

The Snap cards, featuring characters from the Pokémon Snap video game, are exceptionally rare and hold significant value due to their limited release. These cards, such as the Snap #1 card featuring the Pokémon, Todd, are highly sought after by collectors for their scarcity and connection to the beloved Pokémon Snap game.

 

4. Trainer No. 3, 4, and 5

The Trainer No. 3, 4, and 5 cards, also known as the "Tamamushi University Magikarp," "Ivy Pikachu," and "E3 Pikachu" cards, are incredibly rare and hold substantial value within the Pokémon card collecting community. These cards were given out to winners of specific tournaments and events, making them exceedingly difficult to obtain.

 

5. Shining Charizard

The Shining Charizard card, part of the Neo Destiny expansion, is another highly prized card among collectors due to its rarity and stunning design. This holographic card featuring a shimmering Charizard has become a symbol of rarity and prestige within the Pokémon TCG community.

 

6. Pre-Release Raichu

The Pre-Release Raichu card is a legendary piece in the Pokémon card collecting world. With only a handful of these cards in existence, it commands a significant value and remains a coveted item for serious collectors.

 

7. Holographic Shadowless First Edition Machamp

The Shadowless First Edition Machamp card is a rare gem among Pokémon collectors. As one of the earliest print runs of Pokémon cards, the shadowless feature and first edition stamp contribute to its rarity and high value.

 

8. Trainer No. 2

The Trainer No. 2 card, also known as the "Secret Super Battle Mewtwo," is a highly sought-after card due to its limited release and connection to specific events. Its scarcity and historical significance make it a prized addition to any Pokémon card collection.

 

9. Gold Star Espeon

The Gold Star Espeon card, part of the Pokémon EX series, is revered for its rarity and stunning gold star design. With its limited availability and captivating artwork, this card has become a cherished item among Pokémon card enthusiasts.

 

10. Tropical Mega Battle

The Tropical Mega Battle cards, featuring Pikachu, Kangaskhan, and others, are incredibly rare and hold immense value in the Pokémon card collecting community. These cards were exclusive to the winners of the 1999 Pokémon World Championships in Japan, making them exceptionally rare and highly desirable for collectors.

As a sports writer, exploring the world of Pokémon card collecting has been an exhilarating journey. The passion and excitement surrounding these rare and valuable cards mirror the enthusiasm found in traditional sports, making Pokémon card collecting a thrilling and competitive pursuit for fans worldwide.
