All you need to know about Roy Jones Jr.

Roy Jones Jr. is an American professional boxer, actor, and rapper, who has a net worth of $7 million. Roy Jones, Jr. retired after a nearly three-decade professional career in which he won 66 of 75 contests. During his career, he made $55 million from guaranteed prizes, Pay-per-view incentives, and sponsors (before taxes and fees).

Who is Roy Jones Jr?

Roy Jones Jr. was born in Pensacola, Florida, in January 1969. As an amateur, he won the 1984 US National Junior Olympics, as well as the 1986 and 1987 US National Golden Gloves. He also won a silver medal at the 1988 Seoul Olympic Games. He went pro in 1989.

How old is Roy Jones Jr?

He is currently 55 years old.

What is Roy Jones Jr’s net worth?

He is estimated to be worth $7 Million.

What is Roy Jones Jr’s career?

He defeated Bernard Hopkins for the IBF Middleweight Championship in 1993. Jones lost his first fight after 34 wins to Montell Griffin in 1997 when he was disqualified for punching a downed opponent and lost the WBC Light Heavyweight title.

In 2003, he defeated John Ruiz to win the WBA Heavyweight Championship. Jones won over ten championships in 2003 before losing to Antonio Tarver for the second time in 2004. He would lose three fights in a row in 2004-05, two of them to Tarver.

Jones won the vacant WBU Cruiserweight title by defeating Zine Eddine Benmakhlouf in December 2013, bringing his career record to 57-8. Jones is the only boxer who began off as a light middleweight and went on to win a heavyweight title.

His rap album, “Round One: The Album,” was published in 2001. In 2004, his outfit Body Head Bangerz released an album. He has appeared in two Matrix films. Jones has continued to compete, winning the vacant WBF cruiserweight title over Bobby Gunn in February 2017 to improve his career record to 65-9.

Jones has appeared in several films, including The Devil’s Advocate, Cordially Invited, Universal Soldier: A New Dimension, and Southpaw. He has also worked as a boxing trainer and pundit, and he maintains both American and Russian citizenship. He was barred from entering Ukraine in 2015 after meeting with Vladimir Putin to request dual citizenship.

Roy’s highest career purse came from his fight against John Ruiz in March 2003. Jones was guaranteed $10 million plus 60% of the fight’s proceeds. Jones earned a total of $26 million from the fight. He made $6.375 million battling Antonio Tarver. Before battling Tarver, Jones earned between $1 and $2 million every bout.

Roy and Mike Tyson declared in July 2020 that they would be coming out of retirement to face off in a highly unique exhibition battle. The two apparently reached an agreement with a sponsor who contributed $50 million to stage the bout. If all Pay Per View milestones are fulfilled, each fighter could earn up to $10 million from the $50 million prize pool.
