Michael Correll – Former Somerset Captain Is Facing Twenty-Two Charges

Michael Correll is facing twenty-two charges after an investigation revealed he had allegedly engaged in misconduct during his time in law enforcement. The charges against Correll include embezzlement, fraud, and official misconduct.

Correll served as the captain of the Somerset Police Department for several years before his resignation in 2019. As a Police officer, Correll was known for his hard work and dedication.

However, an investigation uncovered evidence of misconduct and corruption. According to the investigation, he had allegedly embezzled funds from the department’s evidence room and engaged in fraudulent activities.

The investigation also revealed that Correll had allegedly used his position of power to intimidate and threaten other officers who had questioned his actions. The charges against Correll are severe and could result in significant penalties.

Embezzlement and fraud charges carry a maximum sentence of twenty years in prison and up to $250,000 in fines. Tampering with evidence charges carries a maximum sentence of five years in prison and up to $10,000 in fines.

Official misconduct charges can result in up to ten years in prison and a $10,000 fine.

Michael Correll Arrest Has Shocked Many

The allegations against Correll have sent shockwaves throughout the Somerset community.

Many residents who knew Correll had expressed disbelief at the charges against him. Some have even defended Correll, arguing that he was a dedicated public servant who would never engage in such behavior.

However, others have pointed out the serious nature of the charges against Correll.

They argue that his alleged actions haven’t only violated the community’s trust and undermined the integrity of the entire Police department.
